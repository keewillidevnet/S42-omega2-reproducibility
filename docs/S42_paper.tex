\begin{abstract}
We study the weight-6 Euler sum
\[
S_{4,2}(x)=\sum_{n\ge1}\frac{H_{n-1}x^n}{n^5},
\]
at dyadic arguments. This corrected revision withdraws the first-version claim of exact closed forms at all three arguments in a 21-constant basis. Those relations plateau near finite residuals and are not certified exact. The corrected result is: an exact closed form for $S_{4,2}(1/2)$ in a 13-element dyadic basis; a certified exact relation for $S_{4,2}(1/4)$ in a depth-2 multiple-polylogarithm basis; and an open status for $S_{4,2}(-1/2)$.
\end{abstract}

\section{Correction note}
The first version used a contaminated 21-element basis containing Clausen constants at $\pi/3$ and omitted the single irreducible depth-2 generator of the weight-6 level-2 space. A small residual at one precision is not evidence of exactness. This revision accepts a relation only when the residual tracks increasing precision and the rational coefficients remain stable.

\section{Definitions}
We use
\[
S_{4,2}(x)=\sum_{n\ge1}\frac{H_{n-1}x^n}{n^5}=\operatorname{Li}_{5,1}(x,1).
\]
For $|x|\le 1/2$, the depth-2 multiple polylogarithms used in this repository are evaluated by direct geometrically convergent summation.

\section{Corrected exact closed form at $x=1/2$}
The implemented 13-element dyadic basis is
\[
\zeta(6),\ \zeta(3)^2,\ \zeta(5)\log2,\ \zeta(3)\log^3 2,\ \pi^2\zeta(3)\log2,\ \pi^4\log^2 2,\ \pi^2\log^4 2,\ \log^6 2,
\]
\[
\operatorname{Li}_6(1/2),\ \operatorname{Li}_5(1/2)\log2,\ \operatorname{Li}_4(1/2)\log^2 2,\ \pi^2\operatorname{Li}_4(1/2),\ S_{4,2}(-1).
\]
The coefficient vector in this order is
\[
-\frac{51}{32},\ -\frac14,\ -\frac1{32},\ -\frac16,\ \frac1{12},\ \frac1{1440},\ \frac1{144},\ -\frac1{240},\ 2,\ 1,\ 0,\ 0,\ -\frac12.
\]

\section{Open status at $x=-1/2$}
No certified closed form for $S_{4,2}(-1/2)$ is implemented. The repository deliberately raises an error rather than evaluating a withdrawn relation.

\section{Reproducibility}
